Research

From biological questions to quantitative mechanisms

I combine experimental biophysics with engineering analysis to determine how physical forces arise within cells and regulate cell function.

01
Biological questions

Cellular & Nuclear Biophysics

My current work investigates how chromatin reorganization and nuclear-envelope mechanics generate the physical conditions for nuclear rupture during NETosis. More broadly, I study how cell and nuclear mechanics influence immune responses, cancer progression, tissue repair, and scar formation.

NETosisChromatinNuclear mechanicsFibroblastsTumor microenvironment

Representative questions

  • How does chromatin decompaction generate intranuclear pressure?
  • What determines the mechanical threshold for nuclear-envelope rupture?
  • How do mechanical cues alter fibroblast phenotype and tissue remodeling?
02
Investigative toolkit

Engineering & Quantitative Approaches

I develop and combine tools that translate dynamic cellular phenomena into measurable physical variables. The goal is not simply to apply engineering methods to biology, but to use quantitative measurements to discriminate between competing mechanisms.

Live-cell microscopyImage analysisMachine learningAFMPhysical modeling

Core approaches

  • Live-cell image analysis for quantifying cellular and nuclear dynamics
  • Machine-learning classification of single-cell morphodynamic phenotypes
  • Quantitative measurement of cellular and nuclear mechanics, forces, and molecular mobility using AFM, traction force microscopy, FRAP, and geometric inference
  • Mechanistic modeling and custom experimental-device development
Live-cell analysis Tracking chromatin organization with quantitative image-derived metrics
Future direction

Mechanobiology as a bridge

My long-term goal is to establish a research program that combines experimental mechanics and mechanics-based modeling to uncover how nuclear architecture, material properties, and force transmission govern cancer-cell migration, cell fate, and therapeutic response.
