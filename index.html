About

Connecting physical principles with cell biology

I am a postdoctoral scholar in Hawa Racine Thiam’s lab at Stanford University. My research combines live-cell microscopy, experimental biophysics, quantitative image analysis, and mechanics-based modeling to investigate how chromatin reorganization and nuclear-envelope mechanics govern nuclear behavior, including nuclear rupture during NETosis.

Trained as a mechanical engineer, I approach cells and nuclei as dynamic material systems whose behavior emerges from coupled changes in force, geometry, and material properties. My broader goal is to uncover the inside-out mechanics of chromatin—how changes in chromatin organization reshape nuclear mechanics and, in turn, influence cellular behavior across different cell states and cell types.

  • AI-enabled quantitative image analysis
  • Mechanics-based modeling of cellular phenomena
